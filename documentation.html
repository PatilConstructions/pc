Detailed Documentation Package

1. Material Documentation

Every major material that goes into your building is documented with proper certifications and traceability:

  • Cement Test Certificates ‚Äî Manufacturer's test certificates for every batch of cement used, showing chemical composition and compressive strength compliance with IS 12269/IS 1489.
  • Steel Mill Certificates ‚Äî Mill test certificates for all reinforcement steel showing grade (Fe 415/Fe 500), chemical analysis, tensile strength, bend test, and batch identification for traceability.
  • Aggregate Test Reports ‚Äî Sieve analysis, specific gravity, water absorption, and impact value tests for coarse and fine aggregates used in concrete.
  • Brick/Block Test Reports ‚Äî Compressive strength, water absorption, and dimensional accuracy test reports for masonry units.
  • Invoice & Delivery Records ‚Äî Purchase invoices, delivery challans, and receipt records for all major materials with date-stamped entries.

2. Construction Test Reports

Tests conducted during construction to verify quality of workmanship:

  • Concrete Cube Test Results ‚Äî 7-day and 28-day compressive strength results for all concrete pours, with cube identification linked to specific structural elements.
  • Soil Investigation Report ‚Äî Bore log data, standard penetration test (SPT) values, and soil bearing capacity at foundation level used for foundation design.
  • Water Tank Leakage Test ‚Äî 24-72 hour water retention test reports for overhead and underground water tanks showing no water level drop.
  • Plumbing Pressure Test ‚Äî Hydrostatic pressure test results for all water supply lines, conducted before concealment.
  • Electrical Test Reports ‚Äî Insulation resistance, earth continuity, and earth electrode resistance test results.
